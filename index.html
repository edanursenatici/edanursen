About Eda

I am a PhD candidate in neurobiology at the Leibniz Institute for Neurobiology (LIN) and Otto-von-Guericke University Magdeburg. My work focuses on learning and memory, with particular emphasis on the genetic and behavioral mechanisms underlying memory formation.

My current research examines associative learning, conditioned inhibition, and reinforcement-related behavioral processes in larval Drosophila, integrating behavioral paradigms with molecular and genetic approaches.

Before my doctoral work, I completed both my M.Sc. and B.Sc. in Biology at Middle East Technical University (METU). My research experience spans neurobiology, disease-related neuroscience, molecular systems biology of learning, and experimental laboratory methods including Western blotting, PCR-based workflows, immunohistochemistry, and cell culture.

Research Interests

Learning and memory, behavioral neurobiology, larval Drosophila, conditioned inhibition, sensory preconditioning, reinforcement learning, genetics of memory formation, and molecular neurobiology.
