About

I study neurobiology of learning and memory with a particular interest in the genetic and behavioral mechanisms underlying memory formation. My recent work examines how associative learning, conditioned inhibition, and reinforcement-related processes can be understood through behavioral assays, molecular methods, and model-organism research. I am especially interested in questions at the intersection of genetics and systems and behavioral neuroscience.

Before my current doctoral work, I completed both my M.Sc. and B.Sc. in Biology at Middle East Technical University (METU). My background includes research experience in neurobiology, disease-related neuroscience, molecular systems biology of learning, and signal transduction.

Research Interests

1. Learning and Memory
Research on the biological basis of learning, memory formation, and reinforcement-related behavioral processes.
2. Larval Drosophila Behavior
Behavioral and connectomic questions in larval Drosophila, including conditioned inhibition, sensory preconditioning, and related paradigms.
3. Molecular Neurobiology
Use of molecular and biochemical techniques such as Western blotting, PCR-based workflows, immunohistochemistry, and cell culture methods.
